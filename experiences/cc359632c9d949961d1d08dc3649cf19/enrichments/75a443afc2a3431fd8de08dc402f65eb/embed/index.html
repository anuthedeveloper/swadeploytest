2. Carassius auratus

Carassius auratus, the Ryukin goldfish, is a selectively bred variety of the common goldfish. Its distinct features include an elongated, egg-shaped body, a pronounced hump behind the head, and a double caudal fin. The Ryukin showcases various color variations, from metallic reds to calico patterns. Popular in ornamental fishkeeping, its genetic traits are manipulated to enhance aesthetic qualities, contributing to its appeal as a prized specimen in the aquarium hobby.

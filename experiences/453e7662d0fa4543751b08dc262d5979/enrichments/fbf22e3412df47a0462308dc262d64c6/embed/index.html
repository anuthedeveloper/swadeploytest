1. Aztec calendar

The Aztec calendar, known as the Sun Stone or Aztec Stone of the Sun, is an intricate and iconic Mesoamerican timekeeping system. It comprises two interlocking cycles – the Tonalpohualli, a 260-day ceremonial calendar, and the Xiuhpohualli, a 365-day agricultural calendar. Richly symbolic, the calendar represents the Aztecs' complex cosmology, including gods, creation myths, and celestial cycles, reflecting their deep cultural and spiritual connection with the cosmos.

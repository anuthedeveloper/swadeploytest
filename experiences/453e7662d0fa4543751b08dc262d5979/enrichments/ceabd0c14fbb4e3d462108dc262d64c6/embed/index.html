1. Cold front

A cold front is a boundary separating colder air masses from warmer ones. As the cold air displaces warmer air, it creates dynamic weather conditions. The lifting of warm air can lead to the formation of clouds, precipitation, and storms. Rapid temperature drops are common when a cold front passes, bringing gusty winds and a change in atmospheric pressure. Cold fronts are integral to the Earth's climate system, influencing regional weather patterns and contributing to meteorological phenomena.

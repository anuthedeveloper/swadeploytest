1. Metal lattice structure

The arrangement of metal atoms in a regular, closely packed structure. This crystalline structure is typical for metals and contributes to their physical properties, such as malleability and ductility. The metal lattice provides a ‘sea’ of electrons that can move freely.

2. Electrons

The small blue spheres represent the free electrons within the metal. These electrons are not bound to any specific atom and can move freely throughout the metal lattice. This movement of electrons is what allows metals to conduct electricity.

3. Metal atoms

The large orange spheres represent the atoms in the metal lattice. Metal atoms are arranged in a regular, repeating pattern known as a crystal lattice, providing the structure and stability of the metal.

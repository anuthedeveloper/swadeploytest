The bonds between oxygen and hydrogen atoms within a single water molecule are covalent bonds. In these bonds, electrons are shared between the oxygen and hydrogen atoms, creating a stable molecule.

The bond angle between the two hydrogen atoms in a water molecule is approximately 104.5 degrees. This angle is due to the repulsion between the lone pairs of electrons on the oxygen atom.
